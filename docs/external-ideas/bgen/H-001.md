# `bgen` may benefit from an idea-generation and hypothesis-validation pipeline that filters for realistic unit economics and supports real-world testing

Single-hypothesis file.

### H-001 — `bgen` may benefit from a pipeline that generates ideas, scores them, and supports real-world hypothesis validation

- Status: `active`
- Created: `2026-04-04`
- Statement:
  - `bgen` may create more real business leverage if it is designed as a system that generates business ideas, expands the strongest candidates into business models, estimates unit economics, and scores them for plausibility before any real-world work begins.
  - the core value may come from turning idea selection into a repeatable pipeline instead of relying on manual brainstorming, intuition, or late-stage evaluation after too much time has already been spent.
  - the second major value may come from supporting live hypothesis validation once a promising idea has been selected, including tracking real observations and updating the model as evidence arrives.
  - the main technical focus may need to be unit economics, because ideas that cannot survive realistic economics checks should be filtered out before more expensive execution work begins.
  - the product may also need a way to record real verification work over time, such as CAC checks, conversion checks, or other market tests, so the idea does not stop at abstract scoring.
- Why it matters:
  - If true, the product is less of an idea generator and more of an idea-selection and validation companion.
  - The biggest leverage would come from rejecting weak ideas early, so effort is concentrated on the few ideas with the highest likelihood of working in the real world.
  - Unit economics realism may be the main constraint, because many attractive ideas fail once margins, acquisition cost, retention, and operating complexity are modeled honestly.
  - A durable workflow for recording validation evidence may matter just as much as the first-pass score, because the idea should improve as real results come in.
- Expected upside:
  - generate a larger candidate pool than a human team could manually produce
  - score ideas with more consistency than ad hoc human judgment
  - force explicit business-model thinking early instead of after enthusiasm has already formed
  - reduce wasted effort by discarding ideas that fail economic realism checks
  - make execution more disciplined by only promoting ideas that clear a repeatable validation threshold
  - capture real-world experiment outcomes in one place instead of losing them in chat, notes, or ad hoc spreadsheets
  - make it easier to compare what the model predicted with what happened in the market
- Expected resource cost:
  - high, because the system has to synthesize ideas, model assumptions, test realism, and manage a multi-stage workflow rather than only produce one-shot suggestions
  - medium-to-high engineering complexity if the product needs separate modules for idea generation, scoring, unit-economics modeling, validation/testing loops, and evidence capture
  - likely highest in the validation layer, since realistic unit economics usually depends on many uncertain assumptions and external signals
  - potentially moderate integration cost if external systems such as Google Ads, analytics, CRMs, or landing-page tools are added later to support validation
- Main risks or where this may be wrong:
  - the system may generate plausible-looking ideas that are still too shallow or too generic to become viable businesses
  - scoring may create false confidence if the evaluation rubric is not grounded in real market data
  - unit economics may be oversimplified if the model ignores acquisition channels, retention curves, fulfillment complexity, or implementation drag
  - automatic testing may be difficult because many business ideas cannot be validated cheaply without real-world experiments
  - the product may become a research and modeling tool rather than a system that actually helps find better businesses
  - external integrations may become the center of gravity if they are not kept subordinate to the idea, model, and validation loop
  - Python may be a reasonable implementation choice, but the language itself is not the key hypothesis
- Alternatives considered:
  - keep the product narrow and build only an idea generator without the downstream validation pipeline
  - make the core product a unit-economics modeling assistant that scores human-supplied ideas instead of generating them
  - separate idea generation, business modeling, validation, and evidence capture into distinct tools instead of one end-to-end system
  - start with lightweight scoring and manual review before attempting automated execution or testing
  - keep the product focused on validation support and avoid broad market automation until the evidence loop is proven useful
- Current confidence:
  - `medium`
- Evidence status:
  - supported by the user's stated target: generate business ideas, develop them, build business models, evaluate them, test them, score them, and then attempt to realize the best ones
  - supported by the emphasis on unit economics realism as the main selection gate
  - not yet validated by actual market experiments, a concrete test harness, or evidence that the pipeline can reliably separate good ideas from merely interesting ones
- Evaluation plan:
  - define the smallest viable pipeline stage breakdown for idea generation, scoring, modeling, and validation
  - choose what “realistic unit economics” means for the first version and which assumptions must be explicit
  - test whether the system can rank ideas better than a simple human review baseline
  - run real validation experiments on a small set of ideas before expanding the automation depth
  - compare an end-to-end assistant against a more modular system that helps with modeling, scoring, and evidence capture
  - verify whether the user can reliably record CAC or other market-test results back into the same workflow without losing context
- Next re-evaluation trigger:
  - after the first concrete spec for the pipeline stages exists
  - or after a small set of candidate ideas is scored and compared against human judgment
