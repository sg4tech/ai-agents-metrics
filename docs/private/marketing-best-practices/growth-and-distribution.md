# Growth and Distribution

How the product reaches people and spreads.

## Canonical sources

- **Seth Godin** — *This Is Marketing*, *Permission Marketing*, *Purple Cow*. Permission marketing: earn the right to talk to people who want to hear from you. Purple Cow: remarkable products market themselves. Smallest viable market: find the minimum group who will care deeply, rather than average-market everyone.
- **Gabriel Weinberg & Justin Mares** — *Traction*. The Bullseye Framework: systematically test 19 distribution channels to find the one or two that move the needle. Most startups under-invest in distribution relative to product.
- **Andrew Chen** — *The Cold Start Problem*. Bootstrapping network effects. The "atomic network" framing: the smallest network in which the product provides value. Cold start strategies: come for the tool, stay for the network.
- **Brian Balfour** (Reforge) — the "4 Fits" essay series. Product-market fit, market-channel fit, channel-model fit, model-market fit. All four must align; products that scale in one segment fail when channel changes.
- **Casey Winters** (ex-Pinterest, Grubhub) — growth loops vs funnels; SEO loops; marketplace growth. Growth is a system, not a funnel.
- **Elena Verna** — PLG motion, self-serve B2B, product-led growth loops.
- **Peter Thiel** — *Zero to One*. Distribution as a strategic question, not an execution detail. "If you can get even a single distribution channel to work, you have a great business."
- **Jim Collins** — *Good to Great*. The flywheel effect: no single defining action, no single moment — consistent pushing in one direction, each turn building on the previous. Applies directly to content + community + distribution loops.
- **Ann Handley & C.C. Chapman** — *Content Rules*. The mechanics of content that earns attention: be human, be useful, share generously, build community.
- **Ben Chestnut** (Mailchimp founder) — email as the owned channel that compounds: unlike social media, the list belongs to you; unlike SEO, it reaches people immediately.

## Core concepts

- **Growth loops, not funnels.** A funnel leaks; an output falls off. A growth loop has a reinvested output — the product of step N becomes the input to step N+1. Compound growth comes from loops, not from fixing funnel leakage.
- **Bullseye framework (Weinberg).** Draw three concentric rings: outer (brainstorm all 19 channels), middle (cheap tests on promising ones), inner (double down on the one or two that work). Most startups jump to the inner ring too fast.
- **19 distribution channels (Weinberg).** Viral marketing, PR, unconventional PR, SEM, social media ads, offline ads, SEO, content marketing, email, engineering as marketing, targeting blogs, business development, sales, affiliate programs, existing platforms, trade shows, offline events, speaking, community building.
- **4 Fits (Balfour).** (1) Product-market fit: customers love it. (2) Market-channel fit: your channels reach your customers. (3) Channel-model fit: your monetization supports the economics of the channel. (4) Model-market fit: your revenue math works at the market's ACV. All four must align — one misfit breaks the system.
- **Atomic network (Chen).** The smallest stable network below which the product has no value; above which it is self-sustaining. Find the atomic network first. Ship to it. Expand from there.
- **PLG (product-led growth).** The product itself is the acquisition, activation, and retention mechanism. Works when: (a) time-to-value is short, (b) value can be demonstrated without a sales rep, (c) the product can spread internally (viral within org).
- **Smallest viable market (Godin).** The minimum number of people who will care enough and who are connected enough to spread. Serving them deeply beats serving everyone poorly.
- **Permission marketing (Godin).** People who opted in are more receptive and more likely to spread. Interrupt marketing degrades; permission marketing compounds.
- **Content flywheel.** A self-reinforcing content system: publish → attract audience → audience engages and shares → new audience arrives → more content produced. Each rotation adds momentum. Unlike a funnel, the flywheel has no endpoint — every satisfied reader is an input to the next rotation. Three stages: Attract (SEO, social, communities), Engage (depth, value, POV), Delight (community, loyalty, advocacy). Works on timescales of 12–18 months, not weeks.
- **Email as owned channel.** Email is the highest-trust owned channel: the subscriber consented, the inbox is not algorithmically filtered against you, the list belongs to you regardless of platform changes. Segmented emails outperform unsegmented by 50%+ CTR. Drip sequences (automated sequences triggered by user actions) outperform one-time blasts on every metric. Subject line is the headline: 28–39 characters, specific over clever.
- **Community-led growth (CLG).** Community becomes a primary driver of acquisition, retention, and expansion. Distinct from community as a support channel — CLG means the community is a growth mechanism: members recruit members, answer each other's questions, produce content that attracts new members. For developer tools: Discord, Reddit, and GitHub Discussions are the primary CLG surfaces.

## Practical principles

- **Distribution beats product more often than intuition suggests.** A great product without distribution gets zero users. A mediocre product with distribution gets feedback and iteration cycles. Invest in distribution earlier and more seriously than feels right.
- **Master one channel before adding a second.** Most teams spread across five channels and own none. Find the channel that works first; exploit it fully; then diversify.
- **The channel constrains the product, not the other way.** If you switch channels, you often need to rebuild the product. Channel-product fit is a hard constraint.
- **"Analysis post" > "promo post."** Sharing a real finding or analysis ("I measured X and found Y") spreads in technical communities. "Check out my tool" does not.
- **Hair-on-fire users first.** The early distribution target is the person with an acute, existing, painful problem. Mild interest does not convert; acute pain does.
- **Channels compound differently.** Paid channels are fast and turn off when budget stops. Content and SEO are slow and compound permanently. Community and word-of-mouth have the highest ceiling and the slowest start. Pick the mix based on your time horizon.
- **Don't start Show HN before social proof.** HN is a one-shot. A weak launch buries the project in search results. Wait for at least one independent cite or user endorsement first.
