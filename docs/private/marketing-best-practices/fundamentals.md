# Marketing Fundamentals

The bedrock frameworks. Not optional reading — these are the vocabulary every marketer must share.

## Canonical sources

- **Philip Kotler** — *Marketing Management* (now in 16th edition). The textbook canon: 4 Ps, STP, customer value, market segmentation. The reference all other marketing frameworks argue with or extend.
- **Alexander Osterwalder & Yves Pigneur** — *Value Proposition Design*. The Value Proposition Canvas: maps customer jobs, pains, and gains against product value creators and pain relievers. The most widely used tool for making "we solve your problem" concrete.
- **E. Jerome McCarthy** — the original 4 Ps (1960). Still the first framework to apply to any new product or launch.
- **Derek Rucker & Nir Schreiber** — the 4 As (Aware, Attitude, Act, Act Again). A customer-journey framing that replaced the linear funnel with a loop.

## Core frameworks

### 4 Ps — the Marketing Mix
The fundamental tactical toolkit. Every GTM plan touches all four.

| P | Question |
|---|---|
| **Product** | What are we selling? What problem does it solve? What is the whole product? |
| **Price** | What does it cost? What is the pricing model? What does the price signal about the product? |
| **Place** | Where do customers find and buy it? Which channels? |
| **Promotion** | How do we create awareness and convince people to buy? |

Extension to **7 Ps** (for services): adds People (who delivers the service), Process (how), and Physical evidence (what signals quality).

### STP — Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning
The sequence that connects market analysis to a position in the customer's mind.

1. **Segmentation.** Divide the market into groups with meaningfully different needs, behaviours, or willingness to pay. Segments must be: measurable, accessible, substantial, differentiable, and actionable.
2. **Targeting.** Select which segment(s) to serve. Criteria: segment size, growth rate, competitive intensity, fit with your capabilities.
3. **Positioning.** Within the chosen segment, decide how you want to be perceived relative to alternatives. This is the input to Dunford's five-component process.

### AIDA — Awareness, Interest, Desire, Action
The classic copywriting and funnel framework. Describes the mental journey from first exposure to purchase.

- **Awareness:** The prospect doesn't know you exist. Goal: get noticed.
- **Interest:** The prospect is curious. Goal: keep them engaged; explain the problem you solve.
- **Desire:** The prospect wants what you offer. Goal: make the value concrete and personal.
- **Action:** The prospect buys (or signs up, or downloads). Goal: make the next step obvious and frictionless.

AIDA works at every scale: a single email, a landing page, a product launch, a six-month campaign.

### Value Proposition Canvas (Osterwalder)
A structured tool to close the gap between what you build and what the customer actually needs.

Two sides:
- **Customer Profile:** (1) Customer jobs — what are they trying to accomplish? (2) Pains — what annoys, frustrates, or risks them? (3) Gains — what outcomes do they hope for?
- **Value Map:** (1) Products and services — what do you offer? (2) Pain relievers — how do you eliminate specific pains? (3) Gain creators — how do you create specific gains?

**Fit** occurs when your Value Map matches the Customer Profile. Without fit, positioning and copy are built on sand.

### The Marketing Funnel (and why it's a loop)
The traditional funnel: Awareness → Consideration → Decision → Retention → Advocacy.

The modern framing treats it as a **flywheel** (HubSpot / Bezos): Attract → Engage → Delight → (back to Attract via word of mouth). Each satisfied customer reduces CAC for the next one. The flywheel's spin rate is a function of how much friction each stage removes.

## Practical principles

- **Know the framework before arguing with it.** The 4 Ps are often criticized as product-centric. That critique is valid — but only by someone who already knows what the 4 Ps are and where they fall short.
- **STP before tactics.** Promotion decisions (channel, format, budget) are downstream of targeting and positioning. Running ads before STP is targeting everyone, which means targeting no one.
- **AIDA applies to every atomic unit of communication.** An email subject line must create awareness; the first sentence, interest; the body, desire; the CTA, action. A subject line that fails AIDA kills the rest.
- **The Value Proposition Canvas is the most honest mirror in marketing.** If you cannot populate the Customer Profile from real customer language (interviews, support tickets, reviews), your value proposition is invented, not discovered.
- **The funnel leaks by design; the flywheel compounds by design.** Choose which mental model to optimize for. Funnel thinking optimizes conversion rates at each stage. Flywheel thinking optimizes for customers who promote for you.
